Certified Coding Specialist – Physician (CCS-P)

Course Overview

CCS-P certification focuses on physician-based medical coding including CPT, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS Level II coding. This course prepares candidates to accurately code outpatient services, physician documentation, and professional fee services.

What is CCS-P?

CCS-P (Certified Coding Specialist – Physician-based) is a nationally recognized certification offered by AHIMA. It validates expertise in coding services performed by physicians and other healthcare providers in outpatient settings.

CCS-P certified professionals specialize in coding evaluation and management (E/M), surgical procedures, diagnostic services, and compliance with federal coding guidelines.

Importance of CCS-P

  • Recognized advanced-level coding certification
  • High demand in hospitals and physician offices
  • Improves claim accuracy and reduces denials
  • Enhances career growth in professional coding
  • Competitive salary advantage

Responsibilities of a CCS-P Coder

  • Review physician documentation and medical records
  • Assign accurate CPT, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS codes
  • Apply E/M coding guidelines correctly
  • Ensure compliance with federal regulations
  • Prevent undercoding and overcoding
  • Support revenue cycle management
  • Perform internal coding audits

Eligibility

  • Strong CPT & ICD-10-CM Knowledge
  • Medical Coding Background
  • Experience in Physician Coding (Preferred)

Syllabus

  • Evaluation & Management (E/M)
  • Outpatient CPT Coding
  • ICD-10-CM Diagnosis Coding
  • HCPCS Level II Coding
  • Compliance & Auditing

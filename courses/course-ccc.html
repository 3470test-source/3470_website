Certified Cardiology Coder (CCC)

CCC - Overview

The Certified Cardiology Coder (CCC) certification validates expertise in cardiology medical coding including CPT, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS Level II codes. This course prepares candidates to accurately code cardiac procedures, diagnostic services, and interventional cardiology services.

What is a Certified Cardiology Coder (CCC)?

The Certified Cardiology Coder (CCC) is a specialty certification offered by AAPC. It focuses on advanced cardiology coding skills including cardiovascular procedures, interventional cardiology, diagnostic services, and cardiac device implantation.

A CCC-certified professional specializes in coding complex heart-related procedures such as cardiac catheterization, angioplasty, pacemaker insertion, electrophysiology studies, and echocardiography.

Importance of CCC

  • High-demand specialty in hospitals and cardiology clinics
  • Higher salary compared to general medical coding roles
  • Reduces claim denials and improves reimbursement accuracy
  • Provides expertise in complex cardiovascular procedures
  • Enhances career growth opportunities in specialty coding

Responsibilities of a CCC

  • Review cardiology medical records and operative reports
  • Assign accurate CPT, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS codes
  • Apply appropriate modifiers
  • Ensure compliance with coding guidelines and regulations
  • Work with physicians to clarify documentation
  • Prevent undercoding and overcoding issues
  • Support revenue cycle and billing processes

Eligibility

  • Basic CPC Knowledge
  • Medical Coding Background
  • Experience in Cardiology Coding (Preferred)

Syllabus

  • CPT Cardiology Procedures
  • ICD-10-CM Cardiac Diagnosis Coding
